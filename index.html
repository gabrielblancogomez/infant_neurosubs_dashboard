Study Overview

This study examines EEG measures in infants (n=144) from the EEG-IP dataset, using both latent profile analysis (LPA) and hierarchical clustering (HC) to identify distinct neurophysiological subtypes. Three distinct classes were identified through each method, showing different language development trajectories over time.
Latent Profile Analysis (LPA)
A probabilistic approach that identifies latent classes based on multivariate normal distributions of observed variables.
Hierarchical Clustering (HC)
A deterministic method that builds nested clusters by merging or splitting groups based on distance metrics.

Key Finding

Different EEG-based subtypes show distinct language development trajectories and outcomes at 36 months, suggesting that early neurophysiological markers may predict developmental outcomes.
